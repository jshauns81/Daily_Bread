Graphite & Glass

Built from your reaction to Apple's Liquid Glass sheet: the surfaces belong to Apple, the color belongs to Daily Bread. Neutral graphite glass everywhere — no tinted world, no parchment, no mood palette. Color appears only where it means something: the accent on interactive elements, gold on money, red on Help. Clean, professional, and the cheapest direction to build, because every material here is the system's own.

The theme system, reframed
Themes become accents, not worlds

Exactly what you suggested: your five shipped themes stop repainting every surface and become tint choices inside identical graphite — the same way iOS handles system tints. Same card, five themes. Gold and red never move.

Two appearances, one design
Light mode is the same logic on white

Why this direction wins on every axis

Taste: it's the look you pointed at — Apple's own, which never goes out of style. Identity: Daily Bread is still unmistakably itself: teal accent, gold money, gold-glow Approve, red Help, emoji chores — the invariants read even louder against neutral graphite. Themes: your five palettes survive as accent tints (Guadalupe default), one line of SwiftUI (.tint(theme.accent)) instead of a resurfacing system. Engineering: system materials, system dark/light, system typography — the least custom design code of any direction, which means more of the budget goes to the heatmap, the widget, and the moments that matter. Light mode is the same graphite logic on white, free.
